# BATTERY MAINTENANCE GUIDELINES

## Overview
The battery provides starting power and runs all electrical systems when the engine is off. A failing battery is the #1 cause of roadside breakdowns in fleet operations. Unlike many components, batteries often fail suddenly rather than showing a gradual decline.

## Condition Classification

### New
- Recently installed (within last 12 months)
- Cranking voltage: above 12.4V at rest
- Cold cranking amps (CCA): at or above manufacturer rating
- No action required
- Next check: at 12 months

### Good
- Age: 1–3 years since installation
- Cranking voltage: 12.2V – 12.4V
- CCA: 80%+ of rating
- No immediate service needed
- Next check: at next scheduled service or every 6 months

### Weak
- **REPLACEMENT RECOMMENDED SOON**
- Cranking voltage: below 12.2V at rest
- CCA: below 70% of rating
- Symptoms: slow engine crank, dim headlights at idle, dashboard warnings
- Risk: can fail without further warning, especially in cold weather
- **Action: Replace within 2 weeks. Keep jumper cables in the vehicle in the meantime.**

## Expected Battery Lifespan

| Operating Conditions | Typical Lifespan |
|---|---|
| Hot climate (frequent 35°C+) | 2–3 years |
| Moderate climate | 3–5 years |
| Cold climate | 4–6 years |
| Frequent short trips only | reduce above figures by 25% |
| Primarily highway driving | add 1 year to above figures |

## Age-Based Replacement Guidance

- **0–2 years old**: replace only if showing weakness
- **3–4 years old**: test every service visit
- **5+ years old**: replace proactively even if currently functional — failure is imminent

## Typical Service Actions

- **Battery terminal cleaning**: every 6 months (prevents corrosion-related weakness)
- **Voltage/load test**: every service visit on batteries 2+ years old
- **Battery replacement**: when condition is "Weak" or age exceeds 5 years
- **Alternator test**: required any time a battery fails prematurely (under 2 years)

## Decision Matrix

| Battery Status | Vehicle Age | Priority | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weak | Any | HIGH | Within 2 weeks |
| Good | 6+ years | MEDIUM | Test at next service; likely replace within 3 months |
| Good | 3–5 years | LOW | Test annually |
| Good | 0–2 years | NONE | Normal inspection only |
| New | Any | NONE | No action |

## Electric Vehicle Note
For electric vehicles, the "battery" referenced here is the 12V auxiliary battery, not the traction battery. Traction battery diagnostics require manufacturer-specific tools and are outside the scope of routine fleet maintenance.

## Safety Notes
- Never jump-start a visibly swollen or leaking battery — replace immediately
- Dispose of old batteries through certified recyclers only (contains lead and sulfuric acid)
- Cold weather reduces effective battery capacity by 30–50%; plan replacements before winter
