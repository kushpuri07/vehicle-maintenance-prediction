# MAINTENANCE HISTORY AND ACCIDENT HISTORY GUIDE

## Maintenance History Classification

"Maintenance History" is a qualitative rating of how well the vehicle has been cared for historically. It incorporates service regularity, quality of repairs, and owner diligence.

### Good
- Services performed on or ahead of schedule
- No deferred repairs
- Documented service records
- **Interpretation**: Vehicle is a reliable candidate. Rely on current condition indicators only.
- **Action bias**: Standard maintenance schedule is sufficient.

### Average
- Most services performed, some delays
- Minor deferred items (cabin filter overdue, alignment skipped, etc.)
- **Interpretation**: Neither red flag nor clean bill. Inspect closely at next service.
- **Action bias**: Perform any overdue items at next visit; consider catch-up inspection.

### Poor
- Multiple services missed or significantly delayed
- Likely hidden wear not yet surfaced
- **Interpretation**: High probability of compounding issues. Current component ratings may understate true condition.
- **Action bias**: Full diagnostic inspection required before trusting current readings. Plan for higher-than-expected service costs.

## Accident History

Accident history is the count of recorded incidents. Even minor accidents can create long-term hidden damage.

### 0 Accidents
- No recorded incidents
- No accident-related concerns

### 1 Accident
- Minor history
- Verify repair quality if accident was recent and significant
- Check for frame alignment issues if collision was frontal

### 2 Accidents
- Elevated concern for structural issues
- Inspect:
  - Alignment (drifting, uneven tire wear)
  - Frame (panel gaps, door fit)
  - Airbag system (warning lights, deployment history)
  - Suspension geometry
- Schedule full inspection within 1 month if not done recently

### 3+ Accidents
- **Significant concern**
- Vehicle integrity should be verified by a specialist
- Risk of:
  - Hidden frame damage
  - Compromised crash safety in future incidents
  - Accelerated suspension wear
  - Electrical issues from rewiring
- **Action**: Comprehensive body and safety inspection recommended
- For fleet use: evaluate whether vehicle should remain in service

## Combined Interpretation

When maintenance history and accident history are both known:

| Maintenance | Accidents | Risk Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Good | 0 | Ideal — low risk |
| Good | 1 | Low risk, repair quality matters |
| Good | 2+ | Medium — structural concerns even if mechanically sound |
| Average | 0 | Medium — monitor closely |
| Average | 1 | Medium — catch-up inspection + repair verification |
| Average | 2+ | High — full inspection required |
| Poor | 0 | High — hidden wear likely |
| Poor | 1+ | Very High — compound risk; full diagnostic mandatory |

## Owner Type Context

- **First owner**: typically most diligent; service history most reliable
- **Second owner**: service continuity may have gaps during ownership transition
- **Third+ owner**: verify all claimed service records; unrecorded issues more common

## Insurance Premium as a Risk Indicator

Higher insurance premiums often correlate with higher-risk vehicles. In fleet contexts, premium is shaped by:
- Vehicle age
- Accident history
- Owner tier
- Vehicle type

If a vehicle's premium is notably high relative to its class, this may indicate accumulated risk the operator should investigate.

## Decision Triggers

Trigger full inspection when ANY of the following is true:
- Maintenance history is "Poor"
- Accident history is 2 or more
- Vehicle has changed hands twice or more AND has more than 1 accident
- Maintenance history is "Average" AND any component condition is "Worn Out" or "Weak"

## Safety Notes
- Past accidents affect more than just aesthetics — safety systems, structural integrity, and electrical systems can all be compromised
- Always cross-reference owner claims with independent documentation
- In commercial fleet use, accidents involving the same vehicle multiple times may indicate a driver issue rather than vehicle issue — investigate both
