# REPORTED ISSUES TRIAGE GUIDE

## Overview
"Reported Issues" counts the number of problems a driver or operator has flagged on a vehicle since its last service. This is a leading indicator of overall vehicle health and a strong predictor of imminent failures. In fleet analysis, issue count is treated as cumulative evidence of degradation.

## Severity Scale

### 0 Issues — Healthy
- No driver-reported problems
- Vehicle is operating within normal parameters
- Continue with scheduled maintenance only

### 1 Issue — Monitor
- One problem logged
- Likely a minor concern (small noise, a warning light, a minor fluid drip)
- Diagnose at next scheduled service
- No immediate action unless safety-related

### 2 Issues — Elevated Risk
- Multiple problems accumulating
- Pattern suggests a broader maintenance need
- Schedule diagnostic inspection within 2–3 weeks
- Investigate whether issues share a common root cause (e.g., electrical, cooling)

### 3 Issues — High Risk
- Significant accumulation of problems
- Risk of compounding failure (one fault causing another)
- Schedule full inspection within 1 week
- Review all recent issue reports together, not in isolation

### 4 Issues — Very High Risk
- Pattern of failures indicates broader system health concerns
- High probability of a major component reaching end-of-life
- **Action: Full diagnostic and service within 3 days**
- Consider pulling vehicle from active duty until resolved

### 5 Issues — Critical
- **IMMEDIATE SERVICE REQUIRED**
- Vehicle is exhibiting multiple simultaneous faults
- Safety and operational risk are both high
- **Action: Remove from service immediately; complete diagnostic before return to operation**

## Interaction with Other Indicators

Reported issues should always be interpreted alongside component condition:

| Reported Issues | If any component is "Worn Out" or "Weak" | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 1 | Yes | MEDIUM (service the worn component) |
| 2 – 3 | Yes | HIGH (likely related) |
| 4 – 5 | Yes | CRITICAL (major servicing event needed) |
| 0 – 1 | No | LOW |
| 2 – 3 | No | MEDIUM |
| 4 – 5 | No | HIGH (investigate systemic issues) |

## Interaction with Service History

- **High issues + low service history**: vehicle has been neglected; catch-up service required
- **High issues + high service history**: recurring or chronic problems; escalate to specialist diagnostic
- **Low issues + low service history**: fine for now but schedule routine service
- **Low issues + high service history**: well-maintained, healthy vehicle

## Age Context

- A 1-year-old vehicle with 3+ issues may indicate a manufacturing defect or warranty claim opportunity
- A 10-year-old vehicle with 0 issues may simply have under-reporting — inspect proactively

## Typical Diagnostic Actions by Issue Count

| Count | Diagnostic Approach |
|---|---|
| 0 | Visual walk-around only |
| 1 | Targeted inspection of reported system |
| 2 | Multi-system inspection, OBD-II scan |
| 3 | Full diagnostic scan, road test, fluid analysis |
| 4–5 | Comprehensive inspection, consider component-level diagnostics |

## Safety Notes
- Any single issue that is safety-critical (brakes, steering, airbags) triggers immediate service regardless of total issue count
- Driver-reported issues should always be logged, even if they can't be reproduced on test — intermittent failures often become consistent later
