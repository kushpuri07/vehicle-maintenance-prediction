# VEHICLE AGE AND ODOMETER-BASED SERVICE INTERVALS

## Overview
Vehicle age and total odometer reading are the two strongest passive indicators of maintenance need. A vehicle accumulates wear and component degradation on two dimensions: **time** (even when parked, rubber and fluids degrade) and **distance** (active wear on moving parts).

## Age-Based Service Schedule

### 0–2 years (New)
- Follow manufacturer service schedule exactly
- Warranty typically still active — all repairs should go through authorized service
- Monitor for early-life defects
- Core services: oil/filter change, tire rotation, basic inspection

### 3–5 years (Mid-Life)
- Begin more frequent inspection of wear items (brakes, tires, battery)
- Replace serviceable fluids: brake fluid, coolant, transmission fluid
- Expect first timing belt service (if applicable) around year 4–5
- Typical service interval: every 10,000 km or 6 months

### 6–9 years (Aging)
- All rubber components enter risk zone: hoses, belts, tire sidewalls, bushings
- Battery should be proactively replaced if not already done
- Suspension components (shocks, struts) typically need replacement in this window
- Typical service interval: every 7,500 km or 4 months

### 10+ years (High-Risk)
- Any component can fail unexpectedly
- Cost of ownership typically exceeds alternatives — evaluate replacement vs repair
- Full safety inspection recommended every 3 months
- Rust and corrosion inspection becomes important
- Keep close records — owners may be considering end-of-life decisions

## Odometer-Based Service Milestones

| Odometer Reading | Recommended Services |
|---|---|
| 10,000 km | First oil change, tire rotation |
| 30,000 km | Air filter, cabin filter, brake inspection |
| 50,000 km | Transmission fluid check, brake fluid flush, spark plugs (petrol) |
| 75,000 km | Coolant flush, drive belts, suspension inspection |
| 100,000 km | Timing belt/chain (if applicable), full fluids service, full brake service |
| 150,000 km | Complete drivetrain inspection, consider major component refresh |
| 200,000 km+ | Evaluate each system individually; vehicle is in late life |

## Combined Age + Odometer Risk Assessment

| Age | Odometer | Risk Level | Recommended Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–2 yrs | Any | LOW | Manufacturer schedule |
| 3–5 yrs | Under 60,000 km | LOW | Standard intervals |
| 3–5 yrs | 60,000–120,000 km | MEDIUM | Increased frequency |
| 3–5 yrs | Over 120,000 km | HIGH | Treat as high-use vehicle |
| 6–9 yrs | Under 80,000 km | MEDIUM | Age-based services priority |
| 6–9 yrs | Over 80,000 km | HIGH | Proactive component replacement |
| 10+ yrs | Any | HIGH | Frequent inspection mandatory |

## Typical km/year by Vehicle Type (Reference)

- Motorcycle: 8,000 km/year
- Car (personal): 12,000–15,000 km/year
- SUV: 15,000 km/year
- Van (commercial): 20,000 km/year
- Truck: 25,000–40,000 km/year
- Bus: 30,000–60,000 km/year

If actual odometer is significantly above or below the typical range for the vehicle's type and age, adjust service frequency accordingly.

## Service History Context

"Service History" represents the number of recorded services the vehicle has received.

| Services per Year | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 | Under-serviced; catch-up inspection recommended |
| 1–2 | Minimum maintenance; acceptable for low-use personal vehicles |
| 2–4 | Good maintenance cadence |
| 4+ | Heavy-use vehicle or proactive owner; no concern |

Divide total service count by vehicle age to get this ratio. A 6-year-old vehicle with only 2 recorded services is significantly under-maintained.

## Safety Notes
- Age alone is not a reason to condemn a vehicle — a well-maintained 10-year-old vehicle can outlast a neglected 5-year-old one
- Never skip safety-critical services (brakes, tires, steering) to save cost
- Consider vehicle replacement when annual maintenance cost exceeds 50% of current market value
