# BRAKE SYSTEM MAINTENANCE GUIDELINES

## Overview
The braking system is the single most safety-critical component on a vehicle. A failure in the braking system can result in loss of vehicle control and serious accidents. For this reason, brake condition is the highest-priority maintenance indicator in fleet management.

## Condition Classification

### New
- Brake pads/shoes recently installed
- Pad thickness: 10-12mm (new condition)
- No action required
- Next inspection: at 15,000 km or 12 months, whichever comes first

### Good
- Pads showing normal wear
- Pad thickness: 4-10mm remaining
- No immediate service needed
- Next inspection: at 10,000 km or 6 months, whichever comes first
- Monitor for any new noise, vibration, or pulling during braking

### Worn Out
- **CRITICAL SAFETY ISSUE** — requires immediate service
- Pad thickness: below 3mm (or warning indicator active)
- Risk of brake failure, rotor damage, and extended stopping distance
- **Action: Replace within 48 hours. Do not operate vehicle for long journeys until serviced.**

## Service Intervals by Vehicle Age

| Vehicle Age | Recommended Brake Inspection |
|---|---|
| 0–2 years | Annual inspection |
| 3–5 years | Every 6 months |
| 6+ years | Every 3–4 months |

## Typical Service Actions

- **Brake pad replacement**: required when condition is "Worn Out"
- **Rotor resurfacing or replacement**: required if pads were ignored and rotors scored
- **Brake fluid flush**: every 2 years regardless of condition
- **Caliper inspection**: required on vehicles 5+ years old during any brake service

## Cost Reference (Indian Market, 2025)

- Brake pad replacement (per axle): ₹2,500 – ₹8,000
- Rotor replacement (per axle): ₹4,000 – ₹15,000
- Full brake overhaul: ₹10,000 – ₹25,000

## Decision Matrix

| Brake Condition | Vehicle Age | Reported Issues | Priority | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Worn Out | Any | Any | CRITICAL | Immediate (≤48 hrs) |
| Good | 6+ years | ≥3 | HIGH | Within 2 weeks |
| Good | 0–5 years | ≥3 | MEDIUM | Within 1 month |
| Good | Any | 0–2 | LOW | Next scheduled service |
| New | Any | Any | NONE | No action |

## Safety Notes
- Worn brakes combined with worn tires compounds risk — prioritize brake service first
- Any brake service on heavy vehicles (trucks, buses) should be performed by certified technicians only
- Drivers should report any brake noise, pulling, or soft pedal feel immediately
